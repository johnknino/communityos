What a Seed Leader Does

A seed leader is the first person in their circle to use CommUnity OS. You're not a user — you're a founder. Your role is to connect the platform to real people with real needs, and to hand invite codes to the next five leaders in your community.

🏠
Block Club President
Post neighborhood issues. Track alderman promises. Organize proposals.
⛪
Pastor / Faith Leader
Share resource guides with congregation. Connect families to benefits. Post community needs.
🏥
Clinic Worker / Promotora
Use bilingual health guides with patients. Share the vitals tool. Post health resource needs.
🏫
School Counselor
Connect families to benefits screener. Share school rights guide. Post resource gaps.
🏪
Small Business Owner
Post worker rights info in your shop. Share lending guides. Connect neighbors to each other.
🤝
Mutual Aid Organizer
Post and match needs and offers. Track what your network provides. See what's going unmet.

Your First Two Weeks

Week 1: Explore and Share
Enter your zip code. Browse the knowledge guides that matter to your community. Try the benefits screener. Look up your elected officials. Share one guide with someone who needs it today.
Week 2: Activate Your Circle
Redeem your invite code at onboard.html — you'll receive civic tokens and 3 invite codes of your own. Give those codes to three people you trust: a neighbor, a colleague, someone who shows up. Post the first discussion. Share the first need. Evaluate one official. The community starts with you.
Week 3+: The Platform Learns
As activity grows, the diagnostic engine begins watching for patterns. Unmet needs accumulating? Accountability gaps? The platform cross-references your community's activity with Census income data and CDC health data. Within a month, you'll have a civic health report you can hand to your alderman, your pastor, or a grant application.

What This Is Not

This is not social media. There are no likes, no followers, no feed algorithm. This is civic infrastructure — the boring, essential plumbing that connects people to their rights, their representatives, and each other.

This is not surveillance. There are no accounts, no logins, no data collection. Civic tokens prove you're a community member without revealing who you are. You cannot be tracked, profiled, or identified. In neighborhoods where ICE enforcement is a reality, this distinction is life or death.

This is not a startup. It costs $7 per year to operate. No one profits from your participation. No one is selling your attention. The code is open source. Any community can fork it and run their own.
