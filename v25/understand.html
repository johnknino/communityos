Know How the Systems Around You Work

Where does your money actually go. What can your employer really do. What are you entitled to as a tenant. How does this system work — where does it trap people — and what can you do about it.

Maria found food. But Friday she cashes her paycheck at the place on the corner — $12 gone, every week. That's $624 a year paying for something a bank account does for free. Her landlord raised the rent last month. She doesn't know if that's legal. She signed the lease in English and isn't sure what it says. The credit card she opened to cover a car repair charges 26% interest — she pays $35 a month and the balance doesn't move.

The systems around Maria aren't broken. They're working exactly as designed — for someone else. Understanding how they work is the first step to making them work for her.

Know Your Community

Look up your community's demographics, income, housing, education, and health data. Search by zip code, city, or neighborhood. Updated with 2024 American Community Survey data.

Where federal money goes. Search by zip code, agency, or program. See how much funding your community receives. No API key required.

How healthy is your county compared to others? Rankings for every US county, plus evidence-based strategies for improvement. A tool for communities that want to know what to work on.
