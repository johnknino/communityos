⚡ Why This Exists Core ▶

The One Big Beautiful Bill Act (signed July 4, 2025) enacted the largest cuts to American safety net programs in history: $1 trillion from Medicaid, up to $187 billion from SNAP. Over 8,000 federal web pages and 3,000+ datasets removed or altered. The USDA eliminated its own 30-year hunger survey.

No civic technology platform bridges the gap from crisis survival to community governance. Every existing platform — Findhelp, 211, mRelief, Code for America, Decidim, Loomio, Mutual Aid Hub — occupies one segment. Nobody connects them.

CommUnity OS does. And the research confirms no one else has built it.

📜 Data Governance Charter (CARE + OCAP) Core ▶

Adapted from Carroll et al. (Data Science Journal, 2020) and Canada's OCAP principles.

Collective Benefit: Community data serves communities first. Summaries flow up for resource allocation only — never surveillance or extraction.

Authority to Control: Each community owns their Sheet. They decide what to collect, share, and when to disconnect.

Responsibility: Builder maintains code + docs. Communities maintain local data. Neither owns the other's domain.

Ethics: No personal data flows upward. Only counts, averages, patterns.

Ownership + Possession: Community Sheet owned by community's Google account. Platform holds no copies. Revoke access anytime.

Frontend

21 static HTML pages, bilingual. shared.js (1,129 lines). shared.css. plant_database.json (40 plants, 16 zones). No frameworks. Progressive enhancement.

Backend

Google Apps Script: Code.gs (router), CivicDataHub.gs (26 routes), CivicEscalation.gs (11 functions), CommunityRegistry.gs (routing). All data in Sheets.

Federation (IMPORTRANGE Chain)

Community → Ward → City → County → State → National → Global. Same 32-column Summary row at every level. No code. Spreadsheets referencing spreadsheets. Each level sovereign.

Registry Routing

One Netlify deployment. CommunityRegistry tab maps zip→script_url. New community joins by adding one row.

👥 Roles Core ▶

Community Data Steward

One per community. Curates local pantry hours, crisis resources, intelligence briefings. The person who knows which pantry has diapers this week. First instance of Principle 7.

Builder (Assessment Coordinator Role)

Peer review, not central command. Communities operate. Builder reads the network's output and improves the design. Same complementary capacity model as the Alta Williams partnership. Work scales with pattern density, not community count.

Community Governance Association (Future)

Modeled on Decidim's Free Software Association. Community-governed body that controls platform direction after the builder steps back.
