Connect your data

CommUnity OS already pulls from public data sources — 311 complaints, health statistics, food inspections, housing data. But the most useful information is often held by organizations working directly with communities. If your organization has data that could help families in real time, we can connect it.

How it works

Your organization provides a data feed — an API, a CSV export, or a structured spreadsheet. We build a secure connector that pulls your data into the platform on a schedule you control. Your data shows up on the pages where families need it, in their language, scoped to their zip code. You control what flows. We commit to how it's used: no resale, no ads, no personal data, display only.

Food banks + pantries

Connect your inventory or distribution schedule. When Maria opens the survival page and types her zip code, she sees what's available near her right now — not a directory, but live information. Your data reduces waste by matching supply to demand in real time.

Community health centers

Connect appointment availability or service hours. Families see which clinics near them are taking patients — in their language. For health data that falls under HIPAA, we execute a Business Associate Agreement (BAA) through NinoTech LLC. No protected health information is ever displayed publicly. Only aggregate availability and service types.

Legal aid + tenant organizations

Connect your intake capacity or event calendar. Families who read the tenant rights guide can immediately see whether free legal help is available near them and how to get it. The platform turns knowledge into action by closing the gap between understanding your rights and accessing someone who can enforce them.

Government agencies + public data

We already pull Chicago 311 data, CDC health measures, CFPB complaints, food inspection results, and Census demographics through public APIs. If your agency has additional open data that could help families make better decisions — connect it. The platform is the last mile between your data and the people it's supposed to serve.

Funders: sponsor a data connection

Every API integration makes the platform more useful for the next family that opens it. If your foundation funds food security, housing, health access, or civic engagement — sponsoring a data connection puts your investment directly into community infrastructure that can't be defunded. The platform costs $7 a year. Your sponsorship builds the data layer that makes it intelligent.

Partnership structure

Data partnerships for CommUnity OS are executed through NinoTech LLC (Illinois), which provides the platform's technical infrastructure. Every agreement includes: what data flows, how often it updates, where it displays, and what happens if either party wants to disconnect. For health-related data, we execute a BAA. For all other data, we use a Data Partnership Agreement. Both are simple, plain-language documents — not 40-page contracts.

The platform remains free to every person who uses it. Data partnerships are funded by the connecting organization, a sponsoring funder, or operated at zero cost when the data source is already public.

Community Data Governance Charter

▼

CommUnity OS follows community data governance principles adapted from the CARE Principles for Indigenous Data Governance and the OCAP framework. These commitments apply to every community that joins the network.

Collective Benefit

Data generated by communities serves those communities first. Summary metrics flow upward only to improve resource allocation and make civic patterns visible — never for surveillance, marketing, or extraction.

Authority to Control

Each community owns their data in their own Google Sheet, under their own Google account. They decide what to collect, what to share, and when to disconnect from the network. The platform cannot modify or delete community data.

Responsibility

The platform builder maintains the shared code, the community registry, and the documentation. Communities maintain their own local data — pantry hours, crisis resources, discussion moderation, intelligence briefings. Neither party owns the other's domain.

Ethics

No personal data flows upward in the federation. Only summary metrics: counts, averages, resolution rates, civic pressure scores. No names, no posts, no phone numbers, no addresses. The community decides what "summary" means for their context.

Ownership & Possession

Community data belongs to the community. The platform holds no copies. All API calls are real-time queries — the platform displays data but never stores it. A community can revoke access at any time by changing their Sheet's sharing settings or removing their row from the registry.

Data Steward Responsibilities

Each community designates a Data Steward — a trusted member who maintains the community's Google Sheet. The Data Steward moderates flagged content, verifies resource accuracy, updates local information (pantry hours, crisis numbers), and serves as the community's point of contact with the platform. The Data Steward is not an authority figure — they are a custodian selected by the community. Any community member can request a new Data Steward through the propose tool.

Enforcement

If the platform builder violates any principle in this charter — accessing community data without permission, storing copies of community data, sharing personal data upward in the federation, or modifying community data without authorization — the community's remedy is immediate and complete: change the Sheet's sharing settings. The platform has no stored copy and no backdoor. Access is revocable in one click. The community retains all data. The platform retains none.

Verification

This charter is verifiable by design. Communities can audit every claim: (1) Check the Sheet's sharing settings to confirm only designated accounts have access. (2) Inspect the Apps Script code — it is open source and visible in the Script Editor. (3) Monitor the network tab in their browser to confirm what data leaves the page. (4) Download their entire Sheet as CSV at any time. (5) Compare the federation aggregate Sheet against their local data to verify that only summary metrics flow upward. No principle in this charter requires trust — each can be independently verified by the community.

Health Data Addendum

The Community Health Pulse feature collects voluntary, anonymous vitals contributions. HIPAA does not apply (the platform is not a covered entity). De-identification follows HIPAA Safe Harbor methodology voluntarily: 3-digit ZIP prefix (populations >20,000), age ranges (not exact), no timestamps beyond year-month. The k≥11 suppression threshold exceeds European and Canadian regulatory standards. Health data is never used for insurance underwriting, employment screening, or any commercial purpose. The community owns the aggregate health patterns the same way they own their discussion posts.

Privacy & Terms

▼

What we collect

CommUnity OS does not require an account, email address, phone number, or password. When you use the platform, you choose a display name and enter a zip code. Both are stored in a Google Sheet. Discussion posts, needs, offers, leader checks, assessments, and audits are stored with the display name and zip code you provide. Page view counts are logged by date and page — no IP addresses, no device fingerprints, no tracking cookies.

What we do not collect

No real names (unless you choose to use yours). No email addresses. No phone numbers. No location data beyond the zip code you enter. No cookies for tracking. No analytics profiles. No advertising identifiers. No data is sold, shared with third parties, or used for any purpose other than displaying community content on the platform.

Where data is stored

All community data is stored in Google Sheets within a Google Workspace account managed by NinoTech LLC (Illinois). Google's infrastructure provides encryption at rest and in transit. The platform code runs as static HTML on Netlify with free SSL. No third-party databases, no cloud servers, no vendor that can raise prices or change terms.

Data integrity

Every submission (audit, evaluation, vote, proposal) receives a SHA-256 integrity hash. This hash is returned to you as a receipt and stored alongside your submission. If a record is altered after submission, the hash will no longer match. This provides tamper evidence without requiring a blockchain — though the platform is designed to anchor these hashes on-chain when the community is ready.

Children

CommUnity OS is designed for community use by adults and families. We do not knowingly collect information from children under 13. The platform does not require age verification because it does not collect personal information.

Your rights

Because we don't collect personal information, there is no account to delete. Content you post (discussions, needs, offers, guides) can be flagged for removal by the community or removed by request. To request content removal, email info@comm-unity-os.org with the content description and the display name used.

Health information

Health guides on this platform are educational and do not constitute medical advice. They were written by a physician using peer-reviewed sources and clinical experience, but they are not a substitute for professional medical consultation. In an emergency, call 911. For non-emergency health concerns, contact your doctor or a Federally Qualified Health Center.
