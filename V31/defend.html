Insurance Claim Appeal

This generates an internal appeal letter. If your internal appeal is denied, you can request an external review — that decision is binding on the insurer.

Your right: The ACA guarantees a two-stage appeal. Insurers must respond within 30 days (72 hours for urgent care). External review decisions are binding.
Used to calculate your appeal deadline

Credit Report Dispute

This generates a dispute letter to a credit bureau. Send it certified mail with return receipt. The bureau has 30 days to investigate.

Your right: Under the Fair Credit Reporting Act (15 U.S.C. § 1681), credit bureaus must investigate disputes within 30 days and correct confirmed errors.

Debt Collection Dispute

This generates a written dispute. Once sent within 30 days of first contact, the collector must stop all collection until they prove the debt is yours.

Your right: Under the FDCPA (15 U.S.C. § 1692g), you have 30 days to dispute. The collector must cease collection until they provide verification. They cannot call before 8am or after 9pm.
You have 30 days from this date

Tenant Screening Dispute

If you were denied housing based on a screening report, the landlord must give you the screening company's name. This letter disputes errors in that report.

Your right: Under the FCRA, the screening company has 30 days to investigate your dispute. If they reported information they knew was wrong, you can sue for up to $1,000 per violation.

Medical Billing Dispute

This requests an itemized bill and disputes specific charges. Always get an itemized bill before paying anything over $200.

Your right: The No Surprises Act protects against out-of-network surprise billing. Uninsured patients get good-faith estimates. Nonprofit hospitals must offer financial assistance. Call 1-800-985-3059 for federal dispute resolution.

Employment Background Check Dispute

If you were denied a job based on a background check, the employer must have given you a copy of the report and time to dispute it BEFORE making a final decision.

Your right: Under the FCRA, employers must give you the report and 5+ business days to dispute BEFORE rejecting you. In Illinois, employers with 15+ employees cannot ask about criminal history on applications.
